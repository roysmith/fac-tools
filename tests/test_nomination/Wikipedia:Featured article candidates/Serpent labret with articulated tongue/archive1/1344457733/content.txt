Of the presumably hundreds of thousands of gold objects that the [[Aztec Empire]] produced, fewer than 400 remain. The [[serpent labret with articulated tongue]] is perhaps the finest survivor of the crucibles of the [[Spanish conquest of the Aztec Empire|Spanish conquest]]. It is intricately crafted and—perhaps fittingly for an [[Aztecs|Aztec]] object—evokes a certain level of squeamishness: Few of us, most likely, would like it to project out of a hole in our lower lip. Happily, however, we can instead view it on a slender stand at the [[Metropolitan Museum of Art|Met]].

This is a well-contained article that includes essentially all the research on this striking object. I started it in 2018 and then, more recently, tracked down all the residual sources mentioning the work, and solicited photos of exemplar labrets. The article was given a [[Talk:Serpent labret with articulated tongue/GA1|good-article review]] by {{u|Chiswick Chap}} in 2024, and is ready for review here. --[[User:Usernameunique|Usernameunique]] ([[User talk:Usernameunique|talk]]) 00:55, 19 February 2026 (UTC)
